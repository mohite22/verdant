What We Produce

Tissue Culture
Banana Plants

Tissue culture is the rapid multiplication of plant selections and offers the benefit of converting or expanding plantations quickly and efficiently with superior selections of disease-free plants.

All our banana plants are sourced from our foundation block, established and evaluated with the help of world-renowned banana specialist Dr. John Robinson. Complete traceability from foundation block to grower.

All laboratory varieties are selected for their vigorous growth, high commercial yield potential, plant stability and uniformity.

Availability

3 Stages of Development

Our banana plants are available at three stages — choose the stage that best suits your infrastructure and planting programme.

01 🧪

In Vitro

Laboratory Stage

Tiny plantlets sold in batches of 50 (+ 10% extra). Transported cost-effectively by air freight. Requires the receiver to wean and harden off the plants before field planting.

Suitable for buyers with weaning facilities
02 🌱

In Vivo (5 cm)

Weaning Stage

Plants at 5 cm height, fully weaned and hardened off in our automated greenhouse facilities. Ready for transplanting into the nursery or directly into prepared planting sites.

Most popular export stage
03 🌿

Field-Ready (20 cm)

Nursery Stage

Fully hardened plants at 20 cm, grown through our on-site nursery. These plants are immediately ready for planting in the land — 6 to 8 weeks from the weaning stage.

Ready for direct field planting

Our Selection

Available Varieties

All varieties are selected from our elite foundation block for vigorous growth, high commercial yield potential, plant stability and uniformity.

Standard Cavendish

Williams

Williams is the most widely planted banana variety worldwide. It produces high-quality, well-filled bunches and has excellent keeping quality. Our elite selection is chosen for vigorous growth, uniformity and superior commercial yield potential.

  • High commercial yield potential
  • Excellent bunch fill and fruit quality
  • Uniform plant growth for efficient harvesting
Improved Williams

Asdia

Asdia is an improved selection of Williams with a shorter plant height and a faster cycle, bunch to bunch. The shorter cropping cycles deliver higher yield per hectare per annum, and the shorter plant height makes it much easier to manage, especially in windy conditions.

  • Shorter plant height — easier to manage
  • Faster bunch-to-bunch cycle
  • Higher yield per hectare per annum
TR4 Tolerant

Formosana

A Cavendish banana variety with high tolerance to Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense Tropical Race 4 (Foc TR4), acquired through somaclonal variation by the Taiwan Banana Research Institute. Du Roi holds the exclusive supply licence into Africa and the Middle East.

  • High tolerance to Fusarium TR4
  • Exclusive TBRI licence holder
  • Cavendish-type fruit quality
Cavendish Selection

Nandi

A robust Cavendish selection favoured for its consistent bunch weight, excellent fruit filling and strong plant frame. It performs well across a range of climatic conditions and is popular in East African markets for its adaptability.

  • Consistent, heavy bunch weight
  • Broad climatic adaptability
  • Popular in East African markets
Export-Grade

Chico

A high-quality Cavendish variety selected for its superior fruit appearance, uniformity and excellent export characteristics. Widely cultivated for premium markets requiring top fruit grade and consistent presentation.

  • Superior fruit appearance and uniformity
  • Premium export-grade quality
  • Suitable for premium fresh markets
High-Yield

Apollo

A high-yielding Cavendish selection known for its particularly large bunch size, excellent grade distribution and strong field performance. A popular choice for growers targeting high production volumes in subtropical conditions.

  • Exceptionally large bunch size
  • Excellent grade distribution
  • Ideal for high-volume subtropical production

Our Process

From Mother Block
to Field-Ready Plant

A six-step process over five months — producing 2,000 identical plants from a single sucker with less than 1% mutation rate.

01

Mother Block Selection

Plants sourced from our 4-hectare foundation block — evaluated with world-renowned banana specialist Dr. John Robinson for vigorous growth, high commercial yield, plant stability and uniformity.

02

Meristem Extraction

Suckers are harvested and tissue extracted by skilled technicians in ultra-sterile laminar flow benches. Each piece must contain meristematic tissue to generate new shoots.

03

Controlled Multiplication

Plant material multiplied over five rounds (4 weeks each) in 10 controlled growth rooms — stopping at round five to keep mutation below 1%, ensuring genetic stability.

04

Virus Indexing

Every meristem is virus-indexed by an accredited third-party laboratory for all known banana viruses before multiplication begins — guaranteeing disease-free planting material.

05

Weaning & Hardening

Rooting hormone applied, then plants transplanted into trays in fully automated weaning greenhouses. After 4 weeks, plants reach the 5 cm in vivo stage.

06

Nursery Grow-Out

Plants grown on in our on-site nurseries for 6–8 weeks until field-ready at 20 cm. Full traceability maintained from foundation block to the grower.
